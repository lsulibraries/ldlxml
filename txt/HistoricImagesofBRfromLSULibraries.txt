William F. Tunnard carriage and harness factory ambrotype, circa 1855	unknown	ca. 1855	Tunnard, William F.; W.F. Tunnard (Baton Rouge, La.).; Horse-drawn vehicles--Louisiana--Baton Rouge.; Harness making and trade--Louisiana--Baton Rouge.; Ambrotypes.	This ambrotype shows the three-story brick building of the William F. Tunnard carriage and harness factory, located on the corner of Main and Church streets in Baton Rouge, La.     William F. Tunnard (1809-1871) was a native of New York and, by the 1830s, a coach maker in Newark, N. J. By 1842, he and his family were living in Louisiana. In 1845 he purchased a lot on the corner of Church and Main streets in Baton Rouge where he built his carriage and harness factory, a site dominated today by St. Joseph Cathedral. Tunnard served as a Confederate Major in the Civil War. He is buried in Magnolia Cemetery.		1 ambrotype ; 4 x 5 1/4 in.
